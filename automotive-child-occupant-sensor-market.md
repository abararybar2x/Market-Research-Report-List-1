<p><ul><li>Ultrasonic</li><li>Electric Field</li><li>Capacitive</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Automotive Child Occupant Sensor market comprises three main types: ultrasonic, electric field, and capacitive sensors. Ultrasonic sensors use sound waves to detect the presence and position of children in a vehicle's seats. Electric field sensors deploy electromagnetic fields to identify occupants, adapting to various body types. Capacitive sensors measure changes in capacitance caused by the presence of a child, offering high sensitivity. Each type aims to enhance safety by ensuring children are properly monitored in vehicles, aiding in airbag deployment and other safety features.</p></p>
