Rolling railroad tie plates are produced through a process that shapes metal under heat and pressure, offering durability and strength. Forging tie plates are created by shaping heated metal, resulting in enhanced mechanical properties and resistance to wear. Casting railroad tie plates are formed by pouring molten metal into molds, allowing for complex shapes and designs.
