<p><ul><li>Contact Type</li><li>No-Contact Type</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Digital infrared temperature sensors are categorized into two main types: contact and non-contact. Contact type sensors require direct contact with the object to measure temperature, providing high accuracy and reliability. They are often used in applications like industrial processes and HVAC systems. In contrast, non-contact type sensors measure temperature from a distance using infrared radiation, making them ideal for moving objects or hazardous environments. This type is commonly found in medical applications, food safety, and HVAC monitoring, offering quick and safe temperature assessments.</p></p>
