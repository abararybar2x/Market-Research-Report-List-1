<p><p>The vegetarian capsule materials market comprises three primary types: Hydroxypropyl Methyl Cellulose (HPMC), Pullulan, and Starch. HPMC capsules are derived from plant cellulose and are well-known for their high stability and versatility, appealing to various supplement manufacturers. Pullulan, a polysaccharide, offers excellent oxygen barrier properties and is favored for its transparency and film-forming abilities. Starch capsules, made from natural sources like tapioca, provide a cost-effective and biodegradable alternative, catering to the growing demand for plant-based, sustainable products in the health and wellness industry.</p></p>
